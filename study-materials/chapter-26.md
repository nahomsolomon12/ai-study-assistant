# Chapter 26: Current and Resistance

## ⚡ 1. Electric Current

### Definition
- Electric current is the **rate of flow of charge**:
  \[
  I = \frac{dq}{dt}
  \]

### Key Ideas
- Current is caused by **motion of charge carriers (usually electrons)**
- Direction of current = direction **positive charges would move**
- Measured in **Amperes (A)**

---

## 🔁 2. Conservation of Charge (Junction Rule)

- At any junction:
  \[
  \sum I_{in} = \sum I_{out}
  \]

### Key Insight
- Charge is **conserved**
- Current splits or combines, but total stays constant

---

## 🌊 3. Current Density

### Definition
- Current density (vector):
  \[
  \vec{J} = \frac{I}{A}
  \]

### Relationship
\[
I = \int \vec{J} \cdot d\vec{A}
\]

### Important Concepts
- Direction of \( \vec{J} \) = direction of **positive charge flow**
- Related to drift velocity:
  \[
  J = nqv_d
  \]

---

## 🧱 4. Resistance and Resistivity

### Resistance
\[
R = \frac{V}{I}
\]

### Resistivity
\[
\rho = \frac{RA}{L}
\]

### Resistance of a Wire
\[
R = \rho \frac{L}{A}
\]

### Key Ideas
- Resistance depends on **geometry**
- Resistivity depends on **material**

---

## 🌡️ 5. Temperature Effects

\[
\rho = \rho_0 [1 + \alpha (T - T_0)]
\]

### Insight
- Metals: resistivity **increases with temperature**
- Semiconductors: resistivity **decreases with temperature**

---

## 📏 6. Ohm’s Law

\[
V = IR
\]

### Key Points
- Linear relationship between voltage and current
- Not all materials obey Ohm’s Law

---

## ⚡ 7. Power in Circuits

### General Power
\[
P = IV
\]

### For Resistors
\[
P = I^2 R
\]
\[
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
\]

---

## 🔬 8. Microscopic View

- Electrons move randomly but have **drift velocity**
- Collisions with atoms → resistance
- Mean free time affects conductivity

---

## 🧪 9. Materials

### Conductors
- Many free electrons

### Semiconductors
- Few free electrons
- Conductivity increases with temperature

### Superconductors
- Zero resistance at low temperatures

---

## 🧠 Summary

- Current = flow of charge
- Resistance limits current
- Ohm’s Law links V, I, R
- Power describes energy transfer
